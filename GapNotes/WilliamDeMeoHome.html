Research Interests

Universal Algebra & Lattice Theory

  • Notes relevant to my current work.
  • Ralph Freese (my ph.d. advisor) has a nice universal algebra page; note in particular the universal algebra calculator.
  • Keith Kearnes has a nice universal algebra and lattice theory page; note in particular the list of conferences in gegeral algebra.
  • Algebra Universalis is the primary scholarly journal in this area.
  • DML-CZ (Czech Digital Mathematics Library) has many articles in universal algebra and lattice theory.
  • There is a Yahoo! group in universal algebra.

Group Theory

  • Notes relevant to my current work.
  • ProblemsInGroupsAndRings.pdf contains some problems that appeared on ph.d. comprehensive exams in group theory and ring theory at University of Hawaii. Some solutions are available from my ph.d. exams page.
  • 611_07_G-sets.pdf Professor Lampe's excellent notes on G-sets.
  • GSetsExercises.pdf elaborates on Lampe's notes, with proofs of theorems and solutions to exercises.
  • GAP is a powerful computer program for working with groups, rings, fields and other algebras.
    • GapNotes.pdf my own notes on using GAP (mainly excerpts from parts of the GAP manuals that I have found useful, supplemented by some notes, examples, and experiments of my own).
    • The GAP documentation is pretty good, especially the GAP Reference Manual and XGAP Manual.
    • XGAP is a useful add-on package which can draw nice Hasse diagrams of subgroup lattices.
    • AtlasRep is a GAP package which provides an interface to the ATLAS of Finite Group Representations.
  • Groupprops, the Group Properties Wiki, contains helpful info about groups; e.g. GAP examples of direct products.
  • math.GR is the group theory section of arXiv.org
